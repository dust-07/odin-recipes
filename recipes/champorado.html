Description

Champorado is a comforting, sweet chocolate rice porridge traditionally made during rainy afternoons. It is rich, thick, and perfectly balanced when drizzled with milk.

Ingredients

  • Rice: 1 cup glutinous rice (malagkit), rinsed.
  • Chocolate: 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder (or 3–4 pieces of traditional tableya).
  • Sugar: 1/2 cup brown or white sugar (adjust to preference).
  • Water: 4 cups water.
  • Topping: Evaporated milk or condensed milk for serving.

Steps

  1. Boil the Rice: In a medium pot, combine the rinsed glutinous rice and water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat.
  2. Simmer: Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent the rice from sticking to the bottom, until the grains are soft and translucent.
  3. Add Flavor: Dissolve the cocoa powder in a little warm water first to prevent lumps, then pour it into the pot along with the sugar. Stir continuously for another 5 minutes until the porridge thickens to your desired consistency..
  4. Serve: Ladle into bowls and generously drizzle evaporated or condensed milk on top before eating.
