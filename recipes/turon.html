Description

Turon is a beloved Filipino street food made of sweet Saba bananas wrapped in a crispy, brown-sugar-caramelized spring roll wrapper. It is sweet, crunchy, and incredibly satisfying.

Ingredients

  • Bananas: 4 ripe Saba (cardaba) bananas, sliced in half lengthwise.
  • Sugar: 1/2 cup brown sugar (for coating).
  • Sauce: 1 can (15 oz) red enchilada sauce.
  • Wrappers: 8 pieces of lumpia (spring roll) wrappers.
  • Oil: Vegetable oil for deep frying.
  • Optional: Jackfruit strips (langka) placed inside with the banana.

Steps

  1. Coat the Bananas: Roll each banana half generously in the brown sugar until fully coated.
  2. Wrap: Place a coated banana piece (and a strip of jackfruit, if using) near the bottom edge of a lumpia wrapper. Fold the sides inward, then roll tightly from the bottom up. Seal the top edge with a dab of water.
  3. Fry: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Drop in a tablespoon of brown sugar directly into the oil—it will melt and float.
  4. Caramelize: Carefully add the wrapped rolls into the oil. Fry for about 3–5 minutes per side, turning them so the floating caramelized sugar sticks to the outside of the wrappers.
  5. Drain and Serve: Remove from the oil once they are golden brown and crispy. Drain briefly on a wire rack (avoid paper towels, as the caramel will stick) and serve warm.
